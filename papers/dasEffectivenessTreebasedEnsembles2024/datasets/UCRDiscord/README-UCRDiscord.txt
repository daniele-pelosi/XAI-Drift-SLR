University of California, Irvine Time Series Discords Datasets, Presentation, Paper

source: http://www.cs.ucr.edu/~eamonn/discords/

Eamonn Keogh, Jessica Lin, Ada Fu

This webpage provides some extra details about time series discords. The first paper on time series discords was:

E. Keogh, J. Lin and A. Fu (2005). HOT SAX: Efficiently Finding the Most Unusual Time Series Subsequence. In The Fifth IEEE International Conference on Data Mining.

    Click http://www.cs.ucr.edu/~eamonn/discords/HOT%20SAX%20%20long-ver.pdf for a slightly longer version of the paper. This 10 page version has more experiments, more references and more detailed explanations. 
    Click http://www.cs.ucr.edu/~eamonn/discords/ICDM05_discords.ppt to download a PowerPoint presentation many with extra discord experiments (PDF version)
    Click http://www.cs.ucr.edu/~jessica/sax.htm or http://www.cs.ucr.edu/~eamonn/SAX.htm to learn more about SAX, and to get free SAX code.
